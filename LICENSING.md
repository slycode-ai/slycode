SlyCode is licensed under the [Business Source License 1.1](LICENSE) (BSL 1.1).

## What you can do (free)

- Use SlyCode for personal projects
- Use SlyCode for learning, research, or education
- Evaluate SlyCode for any purpose
- Contribute to open-source projects using SlyCode
- Modify the source code for personal use
- Redistribute copies (the license must stay attached)

## What requires a commercial license

- Use by or on behalf of a company, organization, or any entity with paid employees

If your organization wants to use SlyCode, visit [slycode.ai](https://slycode.ai) for commercial licensing options.

## Automatic open-source conversion

On **March 3, 2029** (or 4 years after the first public release of any given version, whichever is earlier), the code automatically converts to the [Apache License 2.0](https://www.apache.org/licenses/LICENSE-2.0) — a fully permissive open-source license with no restrictions.

This means every version of SlyCode will eventually become fully open source. We use the BSL to protect the project during its early growth while committing to openness long-term.

## FAQ

**I'm a solo developer / freelancer. Can I use SlyCode for free?**
Yes. Individual use is always free, including for freelance work.

**I work at a company. Can I use SlyCode?**
Your company needs a commercial license. Visit [slycode.ai](https://slycode.ai) for details.

**Can I fork SlyCode?**
You can copy and modify the source code. The BSL license terms apply to your fork — including the commercial use restriction.

**Will SlyCode always be source-available?**
Yes. The source code is and will remain publicly available.
